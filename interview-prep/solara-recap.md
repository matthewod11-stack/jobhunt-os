## Why it ended here (honest read)

This was a shape decision above the room, not a chemistry problem. Both calls went well by any read that doesn't include the outcome. But the screen debrief had already named the kill risk: "the climate-experience gap, if other finalists come from solar or adjacent." That is exactly what happened. Dana's questions about installer economics were ones a solar-side candidate answers from experience and Jordan answered from homework, and homework was audible. No self-flagellation warranted: the adjacency case was made and made honestly. It lost to someone who didn't need to make it.

## What to improve (→ corpus/memory)

- **Calibration:** the screen's 8/10 was accurate for the screen and wrong for the process. The debrief named the sector gap as the top risk and then positioning stayed on adjacency anyway. Promoted to memory (feedback_hiring_signal_calibration): when a domain-fit question appears in round one, treat it as the thing to actively defuse in round two, not a note to acknowledge.
