# Cheat sheet

## Stories bank

**The lifecycle rebuild (Brightline Pay).** Six static campaigns became a behavior-triggered system in Braze: $2.1M in new ARR influenced within nine months (Braze-attributed, first-touch on activation). Week-one activation went from 34% to 51% by rebuilding onboarding around the customer's first successful payment. Still running at steady state, owned by a marketer I trained.
**Use when:** biggest win, driving revenue, building systems that outlast you, working from data.

**The 60-day paid social kill (Copper & Pine).** Turned off the entire paid social budget for 60 days to test whether organic and email could hold CAC. It held, the budget stayed dead for two more quarters, and the board memo explaining why became the backbone of the Series A narrative.
**Use when:** a bet that worked, disagreeing with consensus, defending or cutting a budget, thinking like an owner.

## Framing rules

- Laid off in a 40% GTM cut: say it plainly in one sentence, then move forward. Don't over-explain; over-explaining reads as shame.
- Never open with tenure ("11 years"). Open with what changed or what you built.
- Numbers travel with their attribution. In writing, "$2.1M ARR influenced" always carries "(Braze-attributed, first-touch on activation)". Spoken aloud, hold the attribution ready for the follow-up question instead of front-loading it.
- Managing four people is a fact, not a headline. Lead with what the team shipped, mention the team in the mechanism.
