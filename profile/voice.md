# Voice

## Voice rules

- Lead every bullet with the result, then the mechanism. Not "worked on lifecycle emails to drive revenue." Instead: "drove $2.1M in influenced ARR by rebuilding the lifecycle program."
- Keep sentences short. If a sentence needs a semicolon to hold itself together, split it into two.
- Use "plainly:" as a discourse marker when cutting through a euphemism or restating the blunt version of something. It should read as a hinge, not a tic: once or twice a document, not once a paragraph.
- Dry asides are allowed but earned: one per page, not one per line. If every sentence has a joke, none of them land.
- Banned words, no exceptions: passionate, leverage/leveraged, synergy, spearheaded, unlock.
- Prefer concrete nouns over abstractions. "The email program," not "the initiative." "The board memo," not "the deliverable." Name the thing.
- Cut the throat-clearing. No "I'm excited to," no "I believe strongly that," no scene-setting before the point.

## Sample paragraphs

I killed the paid social budget at Copper & Pine for 60 days. Not a test with a safety net. The whole line item, gone, while everyone waited to see what broke. Nothing broke. CAC held on organic and email alone, and the write-up explaining why became the spine of the Series A deck. Plainly: most paid budgets are funding habit, not results, and nobody finds out until they turn it off.

What I want next: a company where marketing owns a number, not a calendar of deliverables. I don't need a big team on day one. I've built one from zero before and I'll do it again. I do need a CEO or a head of product who treats growth as a discipline, not a department that makes things look nice. If the roadmap runs on vibes and the marketing plan runs on quarters, we're not a fit.
